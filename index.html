Overview

What Is the 3 Week Breath Workshop?

This workshop is designed to help you build a more conscious relationship with your breath in a grounded and practical way.

Across three weeks, you will learn how you currently breathe, how breathing patterns influence your nervous system and internal state, and how to use the breath to support regulation, performance, and recovery.

The focus is simple, structured, and directly applicable to everyday life. Rather than using complicated techniques or a one size fits all approach, each session is designed to help you understand the breath more clearly and work with it in a way that feels useful and sustainable.

Who It's For

This workshop may be supportive if you want to:

  • understand how you currently breathe and where you hold tension
  • learn practical tools to regulate stress and anxiety
  • improve focus, endurance, and recovery
  • build a simple breath practice you can apply in daily life
  • develop more awareness of your body and internal state

What You’ll Learn

A clear progression over three weeks.

Week 1

Connect to Your Breath

In the first week, the focus is on awareness.

  • Learn how you currently breathe: where the breath moves in your body and what your natural rhythm looks like
  • Build awareness of physical sensations, tension, and how present or disconnected you feel in your body
  • Take home a simple daily practice: gentle nasal breathing to reconnect and observe without force
Week 2

Regulate Your State

In the second week, the focus shifts to nervous system support and emotional regulation.

  • Learn practical breathing techniques to support your nervous system, reducing stress and anxiety
  • Understand how different breathing patterns influence your internal state and emotional regulation
  • Apply structured exercises you can use in real life situations to shift how you feel quickly and effectively
Week 3

Breath for Performance & Recovery

In the final week, the breath is explored as a tool for output, resilience, and restoration.

  • Explore how breath can enhance physical performance, focus, and recovery
  • Learn techniques to improve endurance, manage fatigue, and optimise output
  • Practice targeted exercises to use breath as a tool for training, recovery, and daily performance
